Virat Kohli is an Indian international cricketer and one of the most renowned cricketers in the world. He was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. Kohli is a right-handed batsman and has represented the Indian national cricket team in all three formats of the game: Test matches, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is).
Virat Kohli made his international career cricket debut on August 18, 2008. He played his first One Day International (ODI) match against Sri Lanka in Dambulla, Sri Lanka.
Kohli's cricketing journey began at a young age, and he quickly rose through the ranks. He captained the Indian Under-19 team to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup. He made his international debut for India in August 2008 in an ODI against Sri Lanka and later played his first Test match in June 2011 against the West Indies.
Throughout his career, Kohli has achieved numerous records and accolades. He is widely regarded as one of the best batsmen in the world. Known for his aggressive playing style, Kohli has an impressive batting average and has scored numerous centuries and double centuries in international cricket.
In 2017, Kohli was appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats, succeeding MS Dhoni. Under his captaincy, the Indian team achieved several significant victories, including a historic Test series win in Australia in 2020-2021.
Kohli has received several prestigious awards for his performances, including the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year on multiple occasions. He has also been named in the ICC ODI Team of the Year and ICC Test Team of the Year multiple times.
Off the field, Virat Kohli is known for his philanthropic work and his entrepreneurial ventures. He is married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, and the couple has a daughter together.
Virat Kohli has scored more than 24700 runs in international cricket thus far and is only the third Indian batsman after Sachin Tendulkar and Rahul Dravid to score over 24000 runs in the international arena. Kohli also stands second in the list of batsmen with the most centuries in international cricket, with 75 tons to his name.
It is to be noted that Kohli has most number of international centuries among active players across the world. While Virat Kohli has scored 46 centuries in the ODI format, he has scored 28 centuries in Test cricket and a lone ton in the shortest form of the game.
